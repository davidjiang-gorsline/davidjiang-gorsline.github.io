About

Math roots, ML craft, and a penchant for playful rigor.

I’m David Jiang-Gorsline, a Toronto-based senior applied scientist and product-first ML engineer. My journey began with combinatorics and mathematical finance at the University of Waterloo, where I graduated as class valedictorian. After earning my M.Sc. in Analytics (LLM focus) from USC with a 3.96 GPA, I joined USC’s Information Sciences Institute and shaved 80% off RoBERTa fine-tuning costs by crafting deterministic libraries that made our research reproducible.

Today, I architect multivariate forecasting pipelines and agentic retrieval-augmented-generation systems for RBC Capital Markets. I lead teams that scale alt-data feature mining to 650k signals across 16×64-core clusters, deploy LLM agents summarizing earnings press releases for 2.5k tickers per quarter (keeping hallucinations under 1%), and maintain seven Kubernetes-hosted analytics apps with 99% uptime. Previously, at Ernst & Young, I built a document-intelligence engine that exceeded 95% accuracy, compressed audit cycles from 16 weeks to 5 days, delivered a secure Vertex AI platform in 9 days, and designed climate-risk engines aligning NGFS/IAM scenarios with ECB tests.

I believe in reproducible AI, transparent research, and bridging the gap between cutting-edge models and high-impact production. When I’m not orchestrating ML pipelines, you’ll find me experimenting with generative art, devouring sci-fi novels, or exploring Toronto’s board-game cafés.
